Mexican Rice Recipe

Written By : Maya Lucero

Recipe Description :

This is a take on Mexican rice from my family. I got this recipe from my mom and my mom got it from her tia.

Mexican Rice History

Rice was brought over to Mexico by spaniard colonizers. The spaniard colonizers got rice from when they traveled to Asia. Today you can now find different variations of the "Mexican rice" in different parts of Mexico. For example you can find "Mexican rice" made with chicken broth in northern Mexico, but in southern Mexico you can find just plain white rice.

  • Ingredients
  • 2 cups of long grain rice
  • 5 cups chicken broth
  • 1 can tomato sauce
  • 1 onion
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 4 tablespoons of cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup of corn oil
  • 1/2 cup of butter
Directions
  1. Saute onion and garlic in corn oil in a pan on low heat
  2. Add rice when onions start turning clear and continue to stir
  3. When rice looks lightly toasty, add tomato sauce and continue to stir with cumin.
  4. Add 1/2 cup butter with tomato sauce
  5. Add chicken broth and salt and stir once
  6. Cover and let boil for 5 minutes
  7. Lower heat to lowest setting after boiling for 20 minutes
  8. Turn heat off and leave covered to continue cooking with trapped heat for 10 minutes
  9. Uncover and do a light stir to fluff
  10. Finally, serve and enjoy!
